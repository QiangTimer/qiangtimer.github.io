🌱 This series follows firmware work in three aspects:
🔭 Obtain the system to analyze: unpack, recover, and reach a filesystem or binary set worth studying when vendors make that hard.
🔭 Map the attack surface: e.g. exposed services, update paths, IPC interfaces, trust boundaries — what can be reached before talking about impact.
🔭 Vulnerability research and PoC: turn that surface into issues and, where appropriate, constrained proofs; public notes here stay careful and sanitized.


I would like provide interesting findings about firmware analysis to the community.
