    BREIF BIO

    Since March 2012, I have been a Ph.D. student at State Key Laboratory of Information Security1, Institue of Information Engineering (IIE2), University of Chinese Academy of Science (CAS). Before joining IIE, I was studying at Institute of Software, CAS. My Ph.D. dissertation focuses on community-guided techniques for learning models of human behavior that enable mobile systems to better cope with diverse user populations encountered in the real-world. More broadly, my research interests revolve around Machine learning, Mobile computing, and Internet of Finance. I am an experimentalist who likes to build prototype mobile systems based on well-founded computational models and interesting applications via existing smartphone platforms. Results of my research have been published in conferences that focus on wireless network, ubiquitous computing and mobile sensing research. I am interesting for building exciting and appealing smartphone applications.

    I can be reached at qiangcas@gmail.com.
